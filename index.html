What is tax form 2290?

Essentially, tax form 2290 is a Federal Excise Tax imposed on vehicles - with a gross weight of 55,000 pounds or more - operating in public highways.

What are the consequences of not filing?

Not filing means you will be charged 4.5% of the total tax due. That penalty rate will increase monthly for up to 5 months. The IRS also charges monthly interest.

Who needs to file?

You must file tax form 2290 if you own or you operate a heavy vehicle with a gross weight of 55,000 pounds or more. If you have a truck you use for 5,000 miles or less - 7,500 for farm trucks - you are required to file a return, but you won't need to pay the tax.

How long does it take to file - really?

If you have your information available, it will only take a few minutes. Really.

What is Schedule 1?

The stamped Schedule 1 is proof that your 2290 return has been e-filed with the IRS and approved.

When should I filed tax form 2290?

Form 2290 must be filed for the month the heavy vehicle is first used on public highways during the current tax period.
